## Georgia Car Accident Laws: Complete Guide

Georgia is a traditional at-fault state with modified comparative negligence rules. Understanding Georgia's insurance requirements and fault system is essential for the millions of drivers navigating Atlanta's notorious traffic and the state's extensive highway network.

> [!INFO] Georgia uses modified comparative fault with a 50% bar. If you're 50% or more at fault for an accident, you cannot recover any damages.

## Georgia's Fault System

### Modified Comparative Negligence (50% Rule)

Georgia follows modified comparative fault:
- Your recovery is reduced by your fault percentage
- If you're 50% or more at fault, you recover nothing
- If you're 49% or less at fault, you can recover

**Examples:**
- 20% at fault, $100,000 damages: Recover $80,000
- 49% at fault, $100,000 damages: Recover $51,000
- 50% at fault, $100,000 damages: Recover $0

### The 50% Threshold

Georgia's rule is particularly strict:
- At exactly 50%, you recover nothing
- This differs from 51% bar states
- One percentage point can mean total loss
- Fault determination is critical

### How Fault Is Determined

Georgia considers:
- Police reports and officer statements
- Witness testimony
- Physical evidence
- Traffic camera footage
- Vehicle damage patterns
- Expert reconstruction (serious cases)

## Georgia Insurance Requirements

### Minimum Liability Coverage

Georgia requires (25/50/25):
- **$25,000** bodily injury per person
- **$50,000** bodily injury per accident
- **$25,000** property damage per accident

### Additional Required Coverage

Georgia also requires:
- Proof of insurance while driving
- SR-22 for high-risk drivers
- Uninsured motorist coverage offered

### Recommended Coverage Levels

Georgia minimums may be inadequate:

| Coverage Type | Minimum | Recommended |
|---------------|---------|-------------|
| Bodily Injury | 25/50 | 100/300 or higher |
| Property Damage | $25,000 | $100,000 |
| Uninsured Motorist | Offered | 100/300 |
| Medical Payments | Optional | $5,000-$10,000 |
| Collision | Optional | Full coverage for newer vehicles |

### Uninsured Motorist Coverage

Georgia UM rules:
- Insurers must offer UM coverage
- Can reject in writing
- About 12% of Georgia drivers uninsured
- Atlanta has higher uninsured rate
- Strongly recommended

## Georgia Statute of Limitations

### Deadlines to File

| Claim Type | Deadline |
|------------|----------|
| Personal injury | 2 years from accident |
| Property damage | 4 years from accident |
| Wrongful death | 2 years from death |
| Government claims | Ante litem notice required |

### Ante Litem Notice (Government Claims)

Claims against Georgia government entities require:
- Written notice within 12 months
- Specific information required
- Different rules for state vs. local
- Must precede any lawsuit
- Missing notice can bar claim entirely

> [!WARNING] For claims against Georgia cities, counties, or the state, you must file an ante litem notice. Requirements vary by entity, so consult an attorney immediately.

## Georgia-Specific Rules

### Reporting Requirements

Georgia law requires:
- Report to police if injury, death, or $500+ damage
- File SR-13 with DDS if required
- Exchange information at scene
- Remain at scene of injury accident

### Georgia's "Super Speeder" Law

Excessive speeding has consequences:
- 75 mph+ on two-lane roads
- 85 mph+ on any road or highway
- Additional $200 state fee
- Affects fault determination
- Points on license

### Seat Belt Law

Georgia seat belt requirements:
- Primary enforcement
- All front seat occupants
- All rear seat passengers
- Children under 8: proper restraint required
- Seat belt defense allowed (can reduce damages)

### Cell Phone Laws

Georgia Hands-Free Law:
- No handheld phones while driving
- Hands-free devices only
- No texting
- Cannot touch phone while driving
- Violations affect fault determination

### Hit-and-Run Laws

Georgia hit-and-run requirements:
- Must stop at accident scene
- Must render reasonable assistance
- Must provide information
- Felony if serious injury or death
- Can affect insurance claims

## Special Georgia Situations

### Atlanta Traffic Accidents

Atlanta presents unique challenges:
- Heavy congestion (I-285, I-75, I-85)
- High accident rates
- Aggressive driving common
- Complex interchange accidents
- Multiple jurisdictions

### Uninsured Drivers

If hit by uninsured driver in Georgia:
- File UM claim with your insurer (if covered)
- Sue the driver personally
- May be difficult to collect
- Magistrate court up to $15,000
- Georgia has about 12% uninsured rate

### Rideshare Accidents

Uber/Lyft in Georgia:
- App off: driver's personal insurance
- App on, no ride: limited coverage
- Ride in progress: $1M coverage
- File with appropriate policy
- Georgia regulates ride-sharing

### Commercial Vehicle Accidents

Truck accidents in Georgia:
- I-75 and I-85 are major trucking routes
- Higher insurance minimums apply
- Federal regulations may govern
- Multiple parties may be liable
- More severe injuries typical

### Accidents with Government Vehicles

Claims against Georgia government:
- State: notice to Office of Risk Management
- Counties: notice to county commission
- Cities: notice to city clerk
- 12-month notice requirement
- Sovereign immunity limits may apply

## Insurance Claims in Georgia

### Step-by-Step Process

1. **Report accident** to police (if required)
2. **Seek medical treatment** promptly
3. **Report to your insurer** within policy timeframe
4. **Document everything** thoroughly
5. **Get repair estimates** from multiple shops
6. **Review settlement offer** carefully
7. **Negotiate or hire attorney** if needed

### Georgia Insurance Regulations

Georgia's Insurance Commissioner oversees:
- Claim handling practices
- Rate approval
- Consumer complaints
- Bad faith allegations

### Dealing with Low Offers

If the offer is inadequate:
- Request detailed breakdown
- Provide comparable values
- Get independent estimates
- Document all losses
- Consider hiring attorney
- File complaint with Commissioner

## Georgia Damage Caps

### Punitive Damages

Georgia caps punitive damages:
- Generally capped at $250,000
- Exceptions for drunk driving, intent to harm
- 75% goes to state treasury
- Requires "clear and convincing" evidence

### Medical Malpractice Caps

Note: Auto accident injuries treated by doctors:
- No cap on economic damages
- No cap on non-economic damages for auto accidents
- Malpractice caps don't apply to car crash claims

### Intersection Accidents

Georgia intersection rules:
- Right-of-way laws apply
- Red light cameras in some areas

- Left turns must yield

Georgia pedestrian rules:
- Drivers must yield in crosswalks
- Pedestrians have duties too
- Georgia has high pedestrian fatality rate
- Atlanta particularly dangerous for pedestrians
